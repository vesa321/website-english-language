English grammar


English grammar is the set of rules that govern how words are used in the English language. It helps us structure sentences, use the right tenses, form questions, and make sure our language is clear and understandable. Here's a brief overview of the main components of English grammar:

Parts of speech

Tenses

Sentence structure

Articles

Modifiers

Questions and negatives

Subject-Verb agreement

Punctuation

English grammar provides a framework for clear communication, helping speakers and writers express their thoughts in a structured way. While learning grammar rules is essential, it's also important to practice through speaking and writing to gain fluency and confidence. Understanding the fundamentals of English grammar will give you a solid foundation for mastering the language.

Tenses

Present tense

The present tense is a grammatical tense used to describe actions or situations that are happening right now or are habitual, general truths, or states of being.

Past tense

The past tense is used to describe actions or events that have already happened or were completed at some point in the past.


Future tense

The future tense is used to describe actions or events that will happen after the present moment.
